Lesedi

Head Coach & Founder · Training in Switzerland

Lesedi is the founder of Rolling Thunder Grappling and one of the first Motswana to earn a BJJ black belt. He discovered the art while living and working in Switzerland, where he trained for over a decade under a direct lineage from Brazil.

Returning home to Gaborone in 2016, Lesedi had a simple goal: share what he had learned with his country. to form the creation of a proper club.

His coaching philosophy centres on building fundamentals before anything else. "If your basics are solid, the rest of BJJ follows naturally," he says. "We don't rush anyone."

Belt Ranking

The BJJ Belt System

BJJ has five adult belt levels. Unlike many martial arts, progression is slow and deliberate, the average black belt takes 10–15 years of consistent training.

Belts are awarded by your head coach based on technical skill, mat time, attitude, and competitive performance. There are no belt tests at Rolling Thunder, your game speaks for itself.

White Belt
Beginner — typically 1–2 years
Blue Belt
Intermediate — typically 2–3 years at white
Purple Belt
Advanced — typically 2–4 years at blue
Brown Belt
Expert — typically 2–3 years at purple
Black Belt
Master — average 10–15 years total

Each belt also has up to four stripe promotions. Stripes recognise progress within a belt level.
