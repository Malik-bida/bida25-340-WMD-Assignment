About Us

Our Story

From a Dream to Gaborone's Mat

Rolling Thunder Grappling was founded by Lesedi, a Motswana who discovered Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu while living in Switzerland. After training under a world-class lineage for several years, Lesedi returned home with a single mission: bring BJJ to Botswana.

What started as a small group training in 2018 has grown into Botswana's only dedicated grappling club. We now train in a proper facility in Gaborone, run structured classes three days a week, and travel to South Africa for regional competitions.

Every belt, every rep, every competition trip is a chapter in a story that's still being written and you're welcome to be part of it.

What We Stand For

Our Values

Technique Over Strength

BJJ was designed so a smaller person can defeat a larger one through leverage and timing. We train that principle every single class, knowledge wins.

Respect & Humility

You tap, you learn, you grow. The mat is an ego-free zone. Every person who walks through our door is treated with respect, from day one to black belt.

Community First

We are building more than a club, we are building a community. Our members support each other on and off the mat, in competition and in daily life.

History

Our Journey

From a handful of enthusiasts to Botswana's only grappling club. every year has added a new chapter to our story.

2017
Club Founded

Lesedi returns from Switzerland and begins teaching BJJ to a small group in Gaborone. Rolling Thunder Grappling is born.

2020
First Proper Training Space

The club secures its first facility with mats. Classes become structured and attendance grows week by week.

2020
First Competition Trip

Five members travel to Johannesburg for their first BJJ competition. Rolling Thunder marks its place on the regional map.

2023
Medal Success in South Africa

Multiple gold and silver medals at South African open tournaments. The club's competition programme hits its stride.

2025
Expanded Schedule & Membership

Four classes per week, No-Gi sessions added, and membership grows past 40 active members. The best chapter is still ahead.
